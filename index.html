01 / 03 AI · Research

Reviewer's Desk

An AI-assisted research paper review tool for grad students and reviewers.

AI / Research Tools

Reviewer's Desk parses a research paper PDF into structured sections — Abstract, Introduction, Method, Experiments, Limitations — and helps draft a peer review covering novelty, methodology, experimental rigor, figure citation hygiene, and writing quality. Every comment in the generated review is anchored to a specific section of the paper, with a clickable chip pointing back to the source.

The figure-audit feature is what makes it unique. It checks whether every figure and table caption in the PDF is actually cited in the main text — a common oversight that authors miss and reviewers often miss too. I caught a missing Figure 4 citation in my brother's pre-submission draft using this exact tool.

Four output modes: Conference (NeurIPS / CVPR / ICML style), Journal (TPAMI / Nature style), Course PR (for graduate seminar reviews), and Self-Review (for authors auditing their own drafts before submission).

What I built
  • →PDF section detection via font-size heuristics + numbered-heading regex
  • →Figure / table caption extraction and citation cross-reference
  • →Five-dimension review scoring (Novelty · Method Clarity · Experimental Strength · Reproducibility · Writing Quality)
  • →Structured reviewer-form output exportable to OpenReview Markdown
  • →System prompt with explicit "do not invent" rules
Tech stack

02 / 03 Civic Tech · India

NagarSetu

The bridge between citizens and city — civic accountability for small-town India.

Civic Tech

NagarSetu lets you photograph a civic issue — a pothole, a broken streetlight, garbage overflow — and auto-routes it to the right municipal department based on issue type and location. The complaint lands on a public accountability map where your neighbors can see it, upvote it, and add supporting evidence. The council can't quietly bury it.

The problem it tackles is specific: in small-town India, civic complaints vanish into WhatsApp groups, are mislabeled or rerouted manually, and die without acknowledgment. NagarSetu makes the complaint public the moment it's submitted — creating social pressure alongside the bureaucratic trail.

Designed for first-time smartphone users: the issue type is auto-detected from the photo, so submission takes under 30 seconds. Tested with grandmothers, as the tagline says.

What I built
  • →AI-powered issue-type detection from photographs
  • →Auto-routing to the correct municipal department by ward
  • →Public accountability map with real-time status pins
  • →Upvote and supporting-evidence system for neighbors
  • →Under-30-second submission flow for low-tech users
Tech stack

03 / 03 Finance · Student Life

Paisa

Personal finance for Indian students — built for how you actually spend money.

Finance

Paisa tracks your spending the way you actually live — voice input in Hindi or English, auto-categorized chai and Maggi, hostel rent from landlord, mess split with roommates, mom's monthly transfer separated from your income. Indian student finance is its own animal, and most budgeting apps are built for people who have salaries.

The core insight: you don't track ₹35 chai because it feels trivial. But ₹35 every day is ₹2,100 a month — which is exactly where the allowance disappears. Paisa catches this automatically. UPI transactions from PhonePe, GPay, and Paytm are detected and imported, so you don't have to log anything manually.

Budget anxiety is the other problem it solves. The day before refill you either check your balance with dread or don't check at all. Paisa gives you a clear "remaining this month" number so the 1st is never a surprise.

What I built
  • →Voice expense logging in Hindi and English
  • →UPI-aware auto-import from PhonePe, GPay, Paytm
  • →Smart categorization for student spend patterns (mess, chai, hostel, splits)
  • →Mom-transfer vs. income separation logic
  • →"Remaining this month" dashboard with budget gauge
Tech stack
